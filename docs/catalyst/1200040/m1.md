## Technical Analysis

The emulator design follows the shape of Conway-era Cardano governance rather than a generic mock ledger:

- The Cardano Developer Portal describes Conway governance as DReps, the Constitutional Committee, and SPOs voting on governance actions with proposal lifecycle, snapshot, ratification, enactment, and expiry behavior. The emulator mirrors those lifecycle concepts through proposal records, epoch snapshots, tallies, ratification, enactment queues, and expiry/refund handling. Source: [Cardano governance](https://developers.cardano.org/docs/governance/cardano-governance/).
- `cardano-cli conway transaction build` treats proposal policy scripts as explicit proposal witnesses with proposal redeemers. The emulator now validates those proposal-policy witnesses with `RedeemerTag.Proposing`, matching the CLI/ledger shape. Source: [Submitting governance actions](https://developers.cardano.org/docs/get-started/cardano-cli/governance/create-governance-actions/).
- CML is a lower-level serialization and transaction-builder library; its docs emphasize protocol-parameter-driven transaction building and deferring exact witness rules to era specs. Blaze's emulator therefore keeps witness validation explicit at the ledger boundary and uses protocol parameters directly in tests/benchmarks. Source: [CML low-level SDK](https://developers.cardano.org/docs/get-started/client-sdks/low-level/cml/).
- Mesh exposes governance transaction-builder flows for DRep registration, vote delegation, and voting. Blaze matches this builder-facing capability while adding an in-process emulator to validate the resulting governance transactions without a live node. Source: [Mesh governance transactions](https://developers.cardano.org/docs/get-started/mesh/transactions-governance/).
- The Conway ledger specification is the normative reference for governance actions, roles, and validation rules. The emulator's coverage is focused on behavior needed by SDK tests: proposal admission, vote validation, tallying, threshold checks, enactment, and witness accounting. Source: [Conway ledger spec](https://intersectmbo.github.io/formal-ledger-specifications/conway-ledger.pdf).
